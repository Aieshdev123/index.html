Top 5 Tourist Destinations in Pakistan

by Web Desk
February 1, 2022
in Landmarks, Tourism
Reading Time: 4 mins read
0
Share on FacebookShare on TwitterLinkedinWhatsapp

In 2020 Pakistan ranked as one of the best places for tourism. Pakistan is being recognized for its beautiful views. The northern areas of Pakistan are very famous for their beautiful and breathtaking views. A person who comes from abroad must visit the northern areas of Pakistan. In the past few years, Pakistan’s tourism has increased a lot. The top 5 tourist destinations of Pakistan are categorized with the synchronization of northern areas, culture, heritage and so much more.

Top 5 Tourist Destinations in Pakistan

Pakistani tourism is a different spectrum of hues, different cultures, a mound of unsynchronized heartbeats. Tourism reflected within Pakistan is more about the breathtaking views, rich old culture, heritage, art and religious architecture. However, the most loved and anticipated part of Pakistans best tourist destinations have been the pinnacles and icy masses and the culturally rich streets of old heritage cities of the country. The top 5 tourist destinations in Pakistan a tourist must visit are;

1. Swat Valley

Swat is located in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa in Pakistan. It is a place that attracts you to nature. There are some places that a person must visit in Swat, such as the Kalam valley, Kumrat valley, Swat museum, and Izmis Lake. Malam Jabba is another famous destination in Swat. Swat’s capital is Saidu Sharif, which reminds us of the Buddhist occurrences in Swat. Mingora Bazar is a convenient site for tourists to say goodbye, prepare for their trip, and do some last-minute shopping before heading to the more adventurous sections of Swat.

2. Hunza Valley

The beautiful views of the Hunza valley attract visitors. In Pakistan’s northern corner, the Hunza Valley shares borders with China and Afghanistan. Another advantage of Hunza is that it allows you to disconnect from reality and from a world dominated by technological devices that have compelled us to stare at screens on our phones and computers. Hunza is the best destination to come to if you need a break. Hunza gives you peace of mind along with breathtaking views. It gives you a unique vibe with its unbelievable beauty.

3. Deosai National Park

The Deosai National Park is located in the western Himalayas of Pakistan, in Gilgit Baltistan (GB). To say the least, the weather in Deosai is unpredictable. While the area is only accessible during the summer, travellers may come across snow even in the middle of the summer. You’d be admiring the wonderful sight of the sun and clouds playing hide-and-seek in the sky one moment and then the next. The sky could be gloomy the next instant, causing chilly winds and thunderstorms.

4. Islamabad

Visitors planning to visit Pakistan’s well-known northern regions nearly always make a pit stop in Islamabad to break up their route. Islamabad also contains some of the oldest universities in the world, which lets us know about the historic value of Islamabad. Some of the places you must visit when you visit Islamabad are the Lok Virsa museum, which means “place of people’s heritage”, the Faisal mosque, and the Monal restaurant, which provides you with a fascinating view while you enjoy your food.

5. Lahore

Lahore is a place that is very rich in culture. We can observe their love for culture through their art, food, and historic ideas. Another thing that is amazing about Lahore is their food, which has a remarkable taste. The Badshahi Mosque was built by Aurangzeb, the Mughal Emperor. When we enter Badshahi Mosque, we see a security line that leads us to the Lahore Fort. The Shahi Qila, or Lahore Fort, is a magnificent and one-of-a-kind Mughal building. The renowned Mahmood of Ghazni built this must-see historical mansion in the 11th century. Another splendid structure, known as the Sheesh Mahal, or Palace of Mirrors, can be seen in the fort’s northwest corner.
